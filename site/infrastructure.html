FP4 WINNER
GB300

$0.18 / hr per FP4 PFLOP — the cheapest dense throughput on the market, narrowly beating TPU v7 internal at $0.28.

BF16 / 8-BIT WINNER
TPU v7

Internal Google workloads land at $0.56 / hr per BF16 PFLOP — 38% cheaper than GB200, 49% cheaper than GB300.

THE CATCH
16 GB

TPU v7 internal carries 16 GB per logical GPU vs. 288 GB on GB300. Different chip, different workload, different scaling story.

READING THE TABLE: GB300 wins FP4 because Nvidia doubled FP4 throughput generation-over-generation while holding bandwidth and capacity flat — so the FP4 line is essentially a software unlock on the same silicon. TPU v7's external pricing (neocloud) carries a 25% markup vs. internal, which is the floor of what Google is willing to give up to seed external demand.
